# Reward Categories & Justification

## Category Design Principles

Each category must:
1. Support wellness or daily life quality
2. Respect adult dignity
3. Avoid cognitive overload
4. Provide clear value proposition
5. Align with Parkinson's care priorities

---

## Category 1: Health & Wellness Support

### Purpose
**Directly support the user's health management and therapeutic needs.**

### Why It's Appropriate
- Aligns rewards with core therapeutic goals
- Reduces financial barriers to complementary care
- Reinforces that exercise is part of holistic health
- Provides tangible, adult-valued benefits

### Therapeutic Benefit
- Encourages comprehensive health approach
- Reduces isolation by connecting to care community
- Validates importance of self-care investment
- Creates positive association between exercise and wellbeing

### Avoids Infantilization By
- Treating users as informed healthcare consumers
- Offering professional-grade services
- Using clinical terminology appropriately
- Respecting user's role in their care team

### Cognitive Load Management
- Clear health-related icon (üè•)
- Simple category name: "Health & Wellness"
- Sorted by cost (low to high)
- Maximum 6-8 items visible

### Specific Rewards (See REWARD_CATALOG.md)
- Physical therapy session credits
- Massage therapy vouchers
- Nutritionist consultation
- Medication reminder premium features
- Telehealth copay assistance
- Medical alert system subscription

## Category 2: Daily Life Convenience

### Purpose
**Reduce physical burden of daily tasks that Parkinson's makes more challenging.**

### Why It's Appropriate
- Acknowledges real challenges without being patronizing
- Provides practical value immediately
- Reduces caregiver burden indirectly
- Supports independence and autonomy

### Therapeutic Benefit
- Conserves energy for therapeutic exercises
- Reduces stress about daily logistics
- Encourages continued community participation
- Maintains dignity and self-sufficiency

### Avoids Infantilization By
- These are services ALL adults use
- No "special needs" framing
- Professional service providers
- User controls frequency and timing

### Cognitive Load Management
- Clear utility icon (üõí)
- Category name: "Daily Ease"
- Grouped by service type
- Recurring vs one-time clearly labeled

### Specific Rewards
- Grocery delivery credits ($10-$50)
- Pharmacy delivery vouchers
- Ride-share/medical transport credits
- Meal kit delivery subscriptions
- House cleaning service
- Grocery pickup service credits
- Adaptive utensil/equipment vouchers

**Support mental health, stress reduction, and quality rest‚Äîcritical for Parkinson's management.**

### Why It's Appropriate

- Addresses anxiety/depression common in PD

## Category 6: Community & Connection

### Purpose
**Facilitate optional social connection without competition or pressure.**

### Why It's Appropriate
- Isolation is major issue in Parkinson's
- Peer support improves mental health
- Learning from others' experiences valuable
- Community provides hope and normalization

### Therapeutic Benefit
- Reduces depression and anxiety
- Provides practical coping strategies
- Creates accountability (gentle, optional)
- Builds supportive relationships

### Avoids Infantilization By
- Moderated, respectful communities
- No public performance metrics
- Optional participation always
- Focus on mutual support, not advice-giving

### Cognitive Load Management
- Community icon (üë•)

- Clear privacy controls
- One-click join/leave

### Specific Rewards
- Monthly online support group access
- Gentle movement class (live-streamed)
- Parkinson's-specific yoga class series
- Caregiver & patient paired workshop
- Educational webinar series access
- Private forum community membership
- Pen pal matching service

## Anti-Pattern Prevention

### ‚ùå What We Don't Include

**"Premium Currency"**
- No secondary coin types
- No gems, tokens, or special currencies
- No conversion mechanics

**"Limited Time" Rewards**
- No FOMO tactics
- No seasonal exclusives that disappear
- No countdown timers

**"Surprise Boxes"**
- No loot boxes
- No mystery rewards
- No gambling mechanics

**"Status Symbols"**
- No public badges
- No profile decorations others see
- No vanity metrics

**"Childish Content"**
- No cartoon avatars
- No pet/character systems
- No "cutesy" language

- [ ] 80%+ of surveyed users find rewards valuable

- [ ] Approved by movement disorder specialist

- [ ] Screen reader compatible
